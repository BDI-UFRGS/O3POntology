Since they are amounts, they don’t hold unity criteria, but they are ontologically rigid and provide an identity criteria. Earth Materials and BFO Objects are disjoint. They are either solid, fluid, or unconsolidated. We usually observe earth materials when constituting other objects, such as an amount of rock constituting a geological unity or an amount of sand constituting a dune." @en
